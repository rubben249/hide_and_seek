# TAG!
### The Playground Chase

---

## WELCOME TO MAPLEWOOD ELEMENTARY

Every recess at Maplewood Elementary ends the same way: with a game of tag. But not just *any* game. This is **the** game — the one everyone's been talking about all week.

Two kids, each convinced they're the fastest and cleverest in school, have decided to settle it once and for all. They won't run alone. By recruiting classmates — from the studious Mastermind to the fearless Show-off — every corner of the playground becomes part of their strategy. The path loops around the swings, the jungle gym, the basketball court, and the picnic tables.

Tag them first. Win recess forever.

## SIMPLE MODE

> If you are new to the game, start here. Once you are familiar, read the Advanced Mode and Team Variant sections.

### GAME SETUP

**1.** Place the board in the center of the table, **simple mode side face up**.

**2.** Each player chooses a color, takes the matching pawn, and places it on their **home space**.

**3.** Shuffle the kid cards and deal each player **4 cards** face down. Place the remaining cards face-down in a pile in the center — this is the **kid deck**.

**4.** The player who was most recently tagged in a real game of tag goes first.

*The 15 playground perk cards remain in the box. They are used in advanced mode only.*

---

### WINNING THE GAME

You are trying to **tag your opponent** by catching their pawn on the board before they catch yours. To do this, you recruit classmates to run alongside you.

Your progress is shown by your pawn's position relative to your opponent's pawn. If your pawn **reaches or passes** your opponent's pawn, you tag them and **win!**

---

### CARDS IN HAND

Whenever you have fewer than 4 kid cards in hand, draw from the kid deck until you have 4.

---

### GAME TURN

On your turn, perform the following steps in order:

1. **Play**
2. **Recruit**
3. **End**

---

### STEP 1: PLAY

Play **2 cards** from your hand to the center of the table:
- **1 face up**
- **1 face down**

**They must have different card names.**

After playing both cards, draw from the kid deck until you have 4 cards in hand.

> **Optional:** Before playing your cards, you may discard any card from your hand face down (in front of you) and draw a replacement. You can do this multiple times per turn, but only **4 times total** across the entire game.

> *In the rare case that all cards in your hand share the same name, you may play 2 identical cards.*

---

### STEP 2: RECRUIT

Your opponent **must recruit** one of the 2 cards you played. They choose 1 card and place it **face up** in front of them. You then take the remaining card and place it **face up** in front of you.

**Moving your pawn:**

Both players now move their pawn based on the card they just recruited.

Look at the card you recruited and count how many cards with **that same name** you now have in play — including the one you just recruited:

| Cards with that name | Use this icon |
|---|---|
| 1 | Top icon |
| 2 | Middle icon |
| 3 or more | Bottom icon |

Move your pawn **forward (clockwise)** by the number shown. If the number is **negative**, move **backward (counter-clockwise)**. If the icon shows **0** or no number, don't move.

Your opponent does the same with their newly recruited card.

> **Arranging your cards:** Stack multiple copies of the same card name together so you can easily count them at a glance.

---

### STEP 3: END

After both pawns have moved, check **all** of the following conditions:

**A — TAG!**
If a player's pawn position **reaches or exceeds** the opponent's pawn, they tag them and **win the game!**

**B — Mastermind Victory**
If a player has **3 or more Mastermind cards** in play, they **win the game!**

**C — Show-off Disaster**
If a player has **3 or more Show-off cards** in play, they **lose the game.**

If no condition is met, your turn ends and your opponent begins their turn.

---

**Running out of cards:** If neither player has won or lost, the kid deck is empty, and your opponent has **fewer than 2 cards** in hand (they won't be able to play next turn), the game ends immediately. The player whose pawn is **closer** to catching the opponent's pawn wins.

---

**Tie situations — the active player wins** in any of the following cases:

- Both players fulfill a win condition at the same time.
- Both players fulfill a lose condition at the same time.
- A player fulfills both a win and a lose condition simultaneously.
- The "running out of cards" rule triggers and both pawns are equidistant.

---

### REMINDERS & CLARIFICATIONS

- The 2 cards played must have different names — unless all your cards share the same name, in which case you may play 2 identical cards.
- If the kid deck is empty, you cannot discard a card to draw a new one.
- If the kid deck is empty, simply stop drawing and continue playing with your remaining hand.
- There is no upper limit to the number of kid cards you can have in play or in hand.
- If you recruit your 4th, 5th, or 6th copy of the same card name, treat it as your 3rd.
- You can only win or lose during the End step, not during the Recruit step. If your opponent recruits their 3rd Mastermind during Recruit, the game continues until End step — if you catch their pawn on the same turn, the active player wins.
- If you catch your opponent's pawn but it "escapes" forward during the same turn, it does not count as catching it.
- You do not need to land exactly on your opponent's pawn — reaching or exceeding their position is enough.
- If your opponent's pawn moves backward and ends up at or behind your position, that counts as catching it.

## ADVANCED MODE

The advanced mode introduces **Playground Perks** — special cards hidden at key spots around the schoolyard. Landing on one lets you grab an advantage that could change the whole game.

### GAME SETUP

Use the **advanced mode side** of the board. This side has **Playground Spots** *(marked with a ⬡ symbol)* in the corners of the path.

Shuffle the 15 playground perk cards. Place the **top 3 face up** next to the board — this is the **playground supply**.

Place the remaining 12 cards face-down in a pile — this is the **playground deck**. Leave space next to it for a discard pile.

---

### GAME TURN — STEP 2: RECRUIT (ADDITION)

After moving your pawn, if it **lands exactly** on a Playground Spot *(⬡)*, you must choose **1 card** from the playground supply. Take it, then **immediately** replace it with the top card from the playground deck.

**Two types of Playground Perks:**

**⚡ Instant** — Resolve the ability immediately after taking the card. Then place the card in the discard pile.

**∞ Ongoing** — Place the card face up in front of you (in front of your kid cards). Its ability remains active for the rest of the game as long as you have it in play.

---

### ADDITIONAL CLARIFICATIONS FOR PLAYGROUND PERKS

- If your pawn lands on a Playground Spot by moving **backward**, you still take a perk card.
- If your pawn **crosses** a Playground Spot without landing exactly on it, you do not take a card.
- If your pawn was already on a Playground Spot and a card effect doesn't move it, you do not take another card.
- Always replace the taken card from the supply **before resolving** the card's ability.
- If an ability cannot be performed (no valid target, etc.), it is ignored with no effect.
- Ongoing abilities are **not retroactive** — they activate from the moment you acquire them onward.
- Some ongoing abilities trigger when you **recruit** a specific type of kid. This applies no matter how you recruited that kid (including via other card abilities).
- Your **home space** is the board space where your pawn started, matching your color.
- Some ongoing cards add new win conditions during the End step. Tie rules still apply.
- If an instant ability tells you to recruit a kid card, place it in front of you as normal, then move your pawn accordingly. If you then land on another Playground Spot, take another perk.
- If both pawns land on Playground Spots in the same turn, the **active player** takes a card first and resolves it fully. Only then does the other player take theirs.
- If Jungle Gym is in play, draw cards until you have 3 in hand (not 4).
- Removing a kid card from play with **Mind Games** or **Time Out** does not cause any pawn to move.
- You may use **Mind Games** to remove your opponent's 3rd Mastermind if they recruited it during the current Recruit step, thus preventing them from winning.

**YARD MONITOR**
*The supervising teacher shows up — right next to your territory.*

During the End step, if the opponent's pawn is on your home space, you win the game.

---

*A shortcut only you know about.*

When your pawn lands exactly on either home space, move 3 spaces forward.

---

**TREEHOUSE**
*A perfect view of everything happening below.*

After your opponent plays both cards during each Play step, you may peek at their face-down card.

---

**COPYCAT**
*You've been watching them closely. You know all their moves.*

When you recruit a kid with the same name as one currently in your opponent's play area, move 1 additional space forward.

---

**SMARTWATCH**
*Latest tech, unfair advantage.*

At the start of each End step, look at the top card of the kid deck. You may place it on the bottom of the deck instead.

---

**PACK LEADER**
*The lookouts trust you. They report directly to you.*

When you recruit a Lookout, move 1 additional space forward.

---

**GRAND STRATEGY**
*You've thought of everything. Seven kids, seven moves, one perfect plan.*

During the End step, if you have 7 or more different kid card names in play, you win the game.

---

**JUNGLE GYM**
*You've staked out the jungle gym. You're not going anywhere — but it limits your reach.*

Your maximum hand size is reduced to 3. Draw cards from the kid deck until you have 3 in hand. All rules that refer to "4 cards in hand" become 3 for you.

---

**DISTRACTION**
*Point at the sky and shout "What's that?!" — works every time.*

Once per turn, before you play your cards, you may force your opponent to discard 1 card from their hand face down. They do not draw a replacement.

---

### Instant (⚡)

---

**SMOKE BOMB**
*A trick from the science fair — disappear before they can blink.*

Move 1 space forward.

---

**BRIGHT IDEA**
*You've just come up with a plan. A real one this time.*

Draw 2 extra cards from the kid deck. Then discard 2 cards from your hand.

---

**DOUBLE TROUBLE**
*Everyone's getting pulled into this.*

Both you and your opponent each recruit 1 kid card from your respective hands — each with a different name from those you already have in play. Both move your pawns accordingly.

---

**MIND GAMES**
*You know exactly what to say to mess with their head.*

Remove 1 kid card your opponent has in play from the game. This does not cause any pawn to move.

> You may use this to remove your opponent's 3rd Mastermind — preventing them from winning.

---

**NEW KID**
*Someone just transferred in. They're exactly who you need.*

Recruit 1 kid card from your hand with a different name from the ones you already have in play. Move your pawn accordingly.

---

**TIME OUT**
*Step back, rethink, try again.*

Put 1 kid card you have in play back into your hand. You may immediately recruit it again — and move your pawn accordingly.

> Returning a kid this way does not cause any pawn to move.

## TEAM VARIANT

Play TAG! with **3 or 4 players** in two teams. Coordinate with your teammate using limited communication — you may not share what cards you have.

Teammates share one pawn, one set of kids in play, and one set of playground perks. Each teammate has their own hand. Teammates win or lose together.

This variant can be combined with either simple or advanced mode.

---

### GAME SETUP

Divide players into 2 teams. Teammates sit **next to each other**, across from the opposing team.

- **4 players:** Two teams of 2.
- **3 players:** One team of 2, one solo player.

Deal each **player** (not each team) **4 kid cards** in hand.

**You may not show, describe, or hint at the contents of your hand to your teammate.**

---

### STEP 1: PLAY

Agree with your teammate on who plays first. That player plays **1 card face up**. Their teammate then plays **1 card face down**. They must have different names.

After both play, each player draws individually back to 4 cards.

> **Optional:** Before playing a card (no matter if your teammate has already played), you may discard a card to draw a replacement. Each teammate may do this up to **2 times** in the entire game. *(If playing 2v1, the solo player may discard up to 4 times.)*

---

### STEP 2: RECRUIT

The players of the opposing team **agree together** on which card to recruit — the face-up card or the face-down card.

---

### STEP 3: END

**Running out of cards (team variant):** If neither team has won or lost, the kid deck is empty, and any opponent has **no cards** in hand, the game ends. The team closer to catching the opponent's pawn wins.

---

### ADDITIONAL CLARIFICATIONS FOR TEAM VARIANT

- If only 1 card remains in the kid deck at the start of Play, the teammate playing face up draws that last card.
- If all your cards share a name with your teammate's face-up card, you may play an identical card face down.
- If the team's pawn lands on a Playground Spot, teammates agree together on which card to take.
- When playing a 2v1, the solo player may discard up to 4 times; each teammate up to 2 times.

## PLAYER AID — SIMPLE MODE

**SETUP**
Use the side of the board without Playground Spots.
Deal each player 4 kid cards in hand. Rest goes in the kid deck face-down.

**STEP 1 — PLAY**
Play 2 cards face-up and face-down (different names). Draw back to 4.
*Optional: Discard 1 card to draw a new one (max 4 times total).*

**STEP 2 — RECRUIT**
Opponent picks 1 of your 2 cards. You take the other. Both players move their pawns.
- 1 copy → top icon | 2 copies → middle icon | 3+ copies → bottom icon
- Positive = forward (clockwise). Negative = backward. Zero = don't move.

**STEP 3 — END**
Check all conditions:
- A) Catch opponent's pawn → **WIN**
- B) 3 Masterminds in play → **WIN**
- C) 3 Show-offs in play → **LOSE**
*Running out of cards: closest pawn to catching wins. Ties: active player wins.*

---

## PLAYER AID — ADVANCED MODE

Use the side of the board **with** Playground Spots (⬡) in the corners.

Shuffle playground perks, reveal top 3 as the supply. Rest goes in the playground deck.

**TEAM VARIANT**
Deal each player 4 kid cards. Each teammate has their own hand.
Each teammate plays 1 card per turn (one face-up, one face-down). They must differ.
Opposing team agrees on which card to recruit.

**⚡ Instant:** Resolve immediately, then discard.
**∞ Ongoing:** Place in front of your kid cards. Always active.

When your pawn lands **exactly** on a ⬡, take 1 card from the supply and replace it from the deck.
